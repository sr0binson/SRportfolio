About Me

Grounded in tech.
Built through life.

I didn't come up through a CS program. I came up through AOL dial-up, a BBS community, Apple Commodore, and a dad who was an engineer. Computers were just always around — and I was always curious about them.

After years in the workforce using technology across e-commerce, startups, and customer service roles, I stepped back to raise and homeschool my family. I stayed curious the whole time — teaching myself HTML through Google and hand-coding a MySpace page at midnight just because I could.

When it was time to re-enter the workforce, I started at Trader Joe's and worked my way into management — learning operations, people, and how to stay calm under pressure. Then I decided to chase the thing that had always excited me: tech.

I'm now an IT Support Intern at Boise School District — repairing Chromebooks, logging Zendesk tickets, and learning every day. I'm working toward my CompTIA A+ and hoping to grow into an operations or support role on a team full of curious, kind people.
